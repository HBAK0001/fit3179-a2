2019 Australian Federal Election Results Explorer (House of Representatives)

Click on a division to view its data!

Election data was sourced from the AEC Tally Room.

This page was produced by Hayden Baker for FIT3179 Assignment 3 at Monash University.

The 2019 Australian Federal Election was held on 2019/05/18. Up for election were all 151 seats in the House of Representatives, as well as 40 of the 76 senate seats. As a result of this election, the incumbent government remained in power for a third term by securing a majority of the seats in the House of Representatives which can be seen in the heatmap below.

Using the rest of this page, you can view the election results broken down into a variety of categories. Try clicking a division on the map to the left to view the first preferences and the two-party preferred comparison.

Any bars on this page encoded like this that lack an abbreviation are informal votes (ballots that were improperly filled out). These votes do not count toward any candidate.
